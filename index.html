Career

I'm a Master student in Astrophysics and Theoretical Physics at the University of Turin. My main area of interest is Numerics, I'm currently developing a PiC simulation in general geometry to solve Vlasov-Maxwell equations. I want to simulate what happens to plasmas around compact objects like Black Holes or Neutron Stars, with the final goal to understand better magnetic reconnection phenomena in the surrounding of these exotic objects. I'm Creative and laborious, easily fit into a team. Truly fascinated by the multi-disciplinary approach to research.

Since March I have started an Erasmus Traineeship at KU Leuven to do the Thesis, right now I am working from home supervised by Prof. Giovanni Lapenta because of the lockdown.

I chose to carry out my thesis work in the Centre for mathematical Plasma-Astrophysics group at the end of my previous Erasmus program, also in KU Leuven. During the class of Prof. Lapenta on Plasma Dynamics, I coded an explicit Particle-in-Cell (PiC) simulation 1d-3V studying the Weibel and the Filamentation instability, in Newtonian and Relativistic framework. The combined use of computational power and reliable numerical code used to tackle Astrophysical problems really intrigues me.

I have proven to be able to work efficiently and productively in a team, thanks to my Erasmus exchanges. I am a creative hard-worker, interested in multi-disciplinary approach to research. My area of interest is the study of magnetic flux tube instability in general geometry. I am focused on expanding my computational knowledge, diving deep into Numerical Methods and Machine Learning. My passion for Artificial Intelligence and Scientific Coding goes beyond the university path. I co-founded the Machine Learning Journal Club, an open source community based in Turin, now becoming a cultural association, which aims to spread Machine Learning techniques and scientific programming through the university network.

Portfolio

    Simulations and Codes:

    • GR-iPiC2D : General Relativity Particle in Cell simulation in general geometry written in Python, given an arbitrary complex metrics in input the simulation solves the Vlasov-Maxwell equations around a Compact object (Msc. Thesis).
    • Covariant Maxwell solver 3D : General geometry Maxwell eqs. solver on a 3D Yee Lattice.
    • Maxwell eqs. (3D): Maxwell eqs. solvers using a Yee lattice in cartesian geometry, propagation in free space and in a dielectric medium are considered.
    • Transverse Electric mode (2D): This simulation solves the Transverse Electric mode eqs. on a Yee lattice in cartesian geometry.
    • Poisson eqs. (2D): Here the implementations of a poisson solvers using Yee mesh, in a cartesian and cylindrical geometry.
    • Landau Damping: Simulation of one of the most famous topic in plasma physics.
    • Xpoint : Study of an X-point particles acceleration site simulate a magnetic reconnection region (C++).
    • Esplicit PiC 1D : Implementation of a simple 1D electomagnetic explicit Particle in Cell code, C++ version and Python version.
    • Data Science : Collection of notebooks about Data Science, ML, DL.
    • Numerical Astrophysics : This gathering is a part of "Online Crash Course on Numerical Astrophysics" which was hosted by Ferdowsi University of Mashhad, Mashhad, Iran, from July 27 to July 31, 2020.
    • PLUTO : Study of particles acceleration to non-thermal energy made by shocks inside a slab jet (Bsc. Thesis). Visualizations were made by using pyPLUTO by Prof. Bhargav Vaidya.
